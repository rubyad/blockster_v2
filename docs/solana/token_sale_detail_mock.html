Overview

A market structure built for traders

Solana's first generation of DEXes were built for AMM-style swaps. They worked, but they leaked value to MEV bots and made institutional market making impossible. Phoenix is a different machine: an on-chain limit orderbook with the latency profile of a centralized venue.

Every order is a discrete on-chain account. There's no impermanent loss, no toxic flow leak, and no MEV-induced price impact for takers. Market makers post quotes, takers fill them, and the entire matching engine runs at Solana's native block time.

$PHX is the protocol token. Holders earn a share of taker fees, vote on parameter upgrades, and bootstrap liquidity for new markets. The Round 1 sale opens 5% of supply to BUX-tier holders before the public sale at TGE.

Who can participate in Round 1?

Any reader on Blockster who holds at least 1,000 BUX in their wallet at the time of registration. Higher BUX balances unlock larger allocation tiers.

What happens if the round is oversubscribed?

Allocations are pro-rated within each tier. Excess SOL is refunded automatically when the round closes.

What's the lockup?

6-month cliff from TGE, then 18 months of linear daily unlocks. No tokens trade before TGE.

Is this audited?

Phoenix has been audited by OtterSec and Neodyme. Sale contracts audited by Sec3. Reports available in the data room.
